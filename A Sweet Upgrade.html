1st - The SSD

Believe it or not, an SSD is crucial these days. If it were the 2000s', nah! I would not have been emphasizing on this a lot. However, its not and as the complexity of Operating Systems increase, more and more 'write-fetch' operations need to be going on underneath the chasis to keep your system up and running. This is where a traditional HDD fails. Averaging a speed of about 100MBPS and a lower inbuilt cache coupled with slow response times, A typical HDD makes it almost impossible to work on a system without cursing the heck outta it! Admit it, we all have gone through it, haha. This is where an SSD comes to the rescue. SSD's are great for faster load times, faster fetch operations, and mainly a much faster response time. Most SSD's (SATA) today operates at an average of 550MBPS read and 450-500MBPS write. Actual speeds may vary, however its a tad faster than a typical HDD. Lets look at some charts:

As you can see from the above charts, the sequential read/write speeds of SSDs' are far stronger compared to traditional HDDs'. Also, just look at the time taken by each for a data transfer. An SSD always wins. If your laptop has an M.2 slot, its always better as its a space saver inside your machine. Its a cherry on a cake if the slot's an NVME capable one.
